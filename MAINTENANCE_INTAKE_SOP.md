# SOP: Maintenance Issue Intake and Routing

**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-27
**Owner:** Process Owner
**Audience:** Property managers, support staff, and maintenance coordinators

---

## 1. Purpose
This SOP shows how to collect a tenant issue, log it, and send it to the right next step.

## 2. Who Does This
This process is used by the AI intake agent and the property support team.

## 3. Tools You Need
- CRM access
- Email system
- SMS or messaging system
- Maintenance team contact route
- Tenant issue form link with photo upload

## 4. Starting Requirements
Before you start, make sure:
- [ ] The tenant can be identified
- [ ] The property address is available
- [ ] The CRM is ready to save a new case
- [ ] The maintenance escalation route is active

## 5. Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: Ask for the issue
1. Ask the tenant to explain the problem.

**What you should see:** A clear first description of the issue.

### Step 2: Collect key details
1. Ask for the property address.
2. Ask for the tenant name.
3. Ask for the best contact number.
4. Ask when the problem started.

**What you should see:** The tenant record has the basic contact details.

### Step 3: Check urgency
1. Ask if there is danger, damage, or loss of service.

**What you should see:** The case can be marked urgent or non-urgent.

### Step 4: Save the case
1. Enter the issue summary in the CRM.
2. Enter the contact details in the CRM.
3. Enter the urgency level in the CRM.

**What you should see:** A new maintenance case is saved in the CRM.

### Step 5: Confirm receipt
1. Send a confirmation email to the tenant.

**What you should see:** The tenant gets a message that the case was received.

### Step 6: Route urgent cases
1. Send urgent case details to the maintenance team.

**Warning:** Do not leave an urgent leak, power issue, or safety issue waiting in the general queue.

### Step 7: Route non-urgent cases
1. Send the issue form link by SMS or email.

**What you should see:** The tenant gets a link to add more details and upload photos.

### Step 8: Update the record
1. Add the routing action to the CRM case.

**What you should see:** The case shows what happened next and who owns it.

## 6. Quality Check
After finishing, verify:
- [ ] The tenant name and property details are saved
- [ ] The issue summary is clear and easy to read
- [ ] The urgency level is marked
- [ ] The tenant received a confirmation
- [ ] The next action is logged in the CRM

## 7. Common Problems and Fixes

| Problem | Why It Happens | How to Fix |
|---------|---------------|------------|
| The issue sounds unclear | Not enough questions were asked | Ask simple follow-up questions |
| The wrong urgency is chosen | Risk details were missed | Ask about danger, leaks, heat, power, and access |
| The tenant did not get the link | Contact details were wrong | Confirm the email or phone number and resend |
| The team has no context | CRM notes were too short | Add a fuller issue summary before routing |

## 8. Notes
**Assumptions made:**
- The agency already has a CRM
- The team has an email tool and SMS tool
- The issue form link already exists

**Who to ask for help:** Property operations lead or maintenance coordinator
